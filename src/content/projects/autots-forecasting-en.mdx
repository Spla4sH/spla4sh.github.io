## What it's about

A pipeline for forecasting the energy consumption of buildings and households (grid import in kW). It uses [AutoTS](https://github.com/winedarksea/AutoTS), which searches across dozens of model classes with a genetic algorithm to find the best configuration. The sister project to my [Darts pipeline](/en/projects/darts-energy-forecasting): same task, different framework, different degree of automation.

## Architecture

- **Rolling-window forecasting** with configurable horizon and stride (default: 6 hours): trained on January, evaluated on rolling forecasts from February through December
- **Weekly retraining**: AutoTS' genetic search runs regularly, the best models are persisted via the template system (`export_template` / `import_template`) and reused on the next run
- **Probabilistic forecasts**: upper/lower bounds via a 95% prediction interval
- **Six metrics** per run: RMSE, NRMSE, MAPE, Pearson r, interval coverage and interval width
- **SVG plots**: actual vs. forecast with confidence band plus an error-difference plot, generated automatically per dataset

## What I learned

AutoML for forecasting takes model selection off your plate, but not validation design. Horizon, stride and retraining frequency shape the result more than the model class does. Also: prediction interval coverage is an underrated metric. A model whose 95% interval only covers 70% of reality is more dangerous for energy management than one with a slightly higher RMSE.
