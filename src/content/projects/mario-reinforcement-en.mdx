## What it's about

A classic RL setup with a twist: Double DQN versus vanilla DQN, on the NES version of Super Mario Bros — and packaged so you can actually watch the agent learn in real time.

## Highlights

- **Double DQN** reduces the typical DQN overestimation of Q-values: two networks (online and target) where action selection is decoupled from value bootstrapping
- **Experience replay** with prioritised sampling
- **Frame stacking** (4 frames) so the agent can perceive velocity
- **Reward shaping**: forward motion + coins + a penalty for losing lives
- Logs to TensorBoard, regular checkpoints persisted

## What I learned

RL is touchy — the difference between "learns nothing" and "learns cleanly" often comes down to hyperparameters that are hard to justify a priori. Patience pays off: after ~12 hours of training the agent reliably clears World 1-1.
