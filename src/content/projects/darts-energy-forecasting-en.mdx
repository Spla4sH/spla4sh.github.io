## What it's about

As part of my work at Fraunhofer ISE I built load-forecasting pipelines for buildings. The project shows how to use [Darts](https://github.com/unit8co/darts) to reproducibly evaluate different model classes against a strong seasonal baseline (`GlobalNaiveSeasonal`).

## Architecture

- **Rolling-window backtesting**: each model is evaluated across multiple forecast horizons and windows
- **Models**: LightGBM (gradient boosting), NHiTS (Neural Hierarchical Interpolation), TSMixer — against `GlobalNaiveSeasonal` as a non-negotiable baseline
- **Pipeline**: deterministic preprocessing, feature engineering (lag features, calendar features), hyperparameter tuning
- **MAE / RMSE / sMAPE** per horizon in a unified results table

## What I learned

The seasonal baseline is surprisingly strong on energy loads — without it, you'll miss how much of a complex model's "performance" actually comes from the weekly cycle. In forecasting, reproducibility (same seed, same splits, same metric) matters more than a fancy model.
